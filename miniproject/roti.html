The roti is a traditional flatbread from the Indian subcontinent. It is normally eaten with cooked vegetables or curries; it can be used as a carrier for them. It is made most often from wheat flour, cooked on a flat or slightly concave iron griddle called a tawa.it is a staple accompaniment to other foods in many South Asian, Southeast Asian, Caribbean, and Southeast African countries. The word roti comes from the Sanskrit word roṭikā, which means "bread"

Ingredients

  • 1 cup wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Oil to brush
